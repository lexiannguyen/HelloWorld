Kyoto


Kyoto is a city located in Southern Japan, famous for its historical Japanese temples. I visited Kyoto in 2019 and had a great time there. The people were really nice and it was easy to walk or take the metro to different places. We purchased a JR Pass, which basically lets us ride any form of public transport in Japan for the duration of our stay. It saved us money because we would have spent more on tickets for everything, plus I got to get some cool stamps on it. I visited the Fushimi Inari Shrine where there was a long hike to the top, and during June it was hot, raining and I was being eaten alive by mosquitoes since it's in the middle of a forest. Still pretty cool though.

Summary
Aspect Thoughts
Food The ramen was really good, but I was missing vegetables since they don't really serve that many. Fukakusa Rame Toriton is a really good ramen place to go to after visiting the shrine!
Transport So convenient! There's literally a station outside of the Fushimi Inari Taisha shrine and it made travel super easy. There are also local buses you can ride.
Sightseeing/Activities Lots of traditional/older Japanese stuff to see, like the shrines and the Kiyomizu Dera Temple. There are many shops nearby that rent kimonos for travelers to wear and take pictures there as long as you're being respectful :)
Notes There were a lot of mosquitoes in the summer, let's just say my ankle was twice its normal size for that day... Don't forget to check the weather.


We went to this restaurant called Fukakusa Ramen Toriton. The owners were so nice and accommodating, they even let one of the kids in our travel group make ramen noodles with the machine. It's a good walking distance from the Fushimi Inari Taisha shrine, so it was really convenient to eat there. We also visited a Starbucks located in the Kiyomizu area (which is really pretty because of all of the traditional architecture). The Starbucks is called Starbucks Coffee Kyoto Ninenzaka Yasaka Chaya and it's located in a traditional building which was super cool. It was really popular so it's hard to find a spot to sit down, but once you do it's pretty magical. There are also other temples and shrines around the area, and you can get to most of them by walking or taking the bus.
