Los Angeles


Los Angeles/LA is a pretty popular vacation spot for my family. We mainly go for the food and amusement parks, but there are many more things to do. I like the Vietnamese food and dessert, some of my favorite spots are Joe's Italian Ice (dessert), Sup Noodle Bar (pho), Tan Hoang Huong Nakery and Deli (Vietnamese sandwiches, coffee). Joe's is so good, every time I go to LA, I have a Joelatti every day. There are so many things to do in LA, including (but not limited to) amusement parks, museums and food. I like to go to Disneyland and Universal studios when we can get tickets in time. LA also has free museums you can visit like the Getty and the Broad. You can also visit the Hollywood Walk of Fame, K-Town and Little Tokyo, though I didn't really like Hollywood, since it was really crowded and reminds me of the streets of New York or Old Las Vegas. You can also hike to the Hollywood Sign (I recommend following a YouTube video guide and to go during the morning,before it gets too hot) or hang out at the beach and enjoy the sunshine.

Summary
Aspect Thoughts
Food LA has SO MANY food places and options to pick from, just read reviews and you'll probably be fine.
Transport I've never taken public transport in LA, my family and I usually drive there from San Francisco, but the traffic in downtown LA is pretty bad.
Sightseeing/Activities There are lots of activities, as long as you plan them out before so you're not driving halfway across the city, you'll probably find something everyone in your party will enjoy.
Hotels There are many options for hotels in LA, and most of the ones I've stayed at were pretty good.
Notes The hot weather is too much for me, so wearing a hat, sunscreen and bringing an umbrella helps.


Below are some pictures from when I visited the Broad, Universal Studios and Joe's Italian Ice. The Broad has free admission as long as you reserve a spot before hand. It also has an infinity room (room of mirrors with shiny lights so it kinda looks like you're in space), which is a popular spot for pictures and you also have to reserve a spot so you're not standing in line for 45 minutes. Universal Studios and Disneyland are my favorite (and only?) amusemnt parks that I go to, they're pretty fun, though I will say that Universal Studios doesn't have that many fun rides, the main ones are the Revenge of the Mummy, Jurassic World and Harry Potter (only because I LOVE harry potter and it's cool to go to Hogsmeade). Another popular "instagrammable" spot is this art installation called Urban Light, which is just a big group of lamp posts.
